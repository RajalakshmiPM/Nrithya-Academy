About Nrithya Dance Forms

Dance has always been the beautiful way to express our feelings. It is a way of self-help and creativity. Regardless of our age or whether we pursue it as a calling or a hobby, dance affects us all and is within us all. It is the joy of letting go of oneself in harmony with the universe, it is sensation of exploring one’s limitations and overcoming them. Dance is the purity of expression, of emotion, of heart. It is the realisation of self, of other, and oneness with the universe. It is eternal; it is ever present in nature. For some the dance is life itself…

You can learn the following dance forms in Nirthya Academy

Indian classical

Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam also historically called Sadri, is a major genre of Indian classical dance that originated in Tamil Nadu. Traditionally, Bharatanatyam has been a solo dance that was performed exclusively by women, and it expressed South Indian religious themes and spiritual ideas, particularly of Shaivism, Vaishnavism and Shaktism.

Kathak

Kathak is one of the main genres of ancient Indian classical dance and is traditionally regarded bards of North India referred as Kathakars or storytellers. These kathakars wandered around and communicated legendary stories via music, dance and songs quite like the early Greek theatre.

Kuchipudi

The classical Indian dance form originated Andhra Pradesh state and got its name from Kuchipudi village near the Bay of Bengal. Kuchipudi is the most popular traditional dance form in South India.
